# LLM-Wiki — Keyword-Triggered Knowledge

> The missing fourth knowledge layer: committable, project-scoped, surfaced
> only when the operator's message tokens match an entry's triggers. Implements
> **Article VII** of the [Constitution](../constitution) via the feature
> `harness-llm-wiki`.

The operator's recurring question — *"LLM 위키의 영속화는 어떤 과정으로 진행되지?"* —
has four load-bearing answers below. Each H2 maps to a specific section of
`harness/research/harness-llm-wiki.md`; each interactive block is a live
demo of the mechanic it annotates.

---

## 1. The four-layer stack

Claude Code already ships three persistence layers; none is
project-scoped **and** keyword-triggered at once. That gap is the reason
Article VII exists.

<KnowledgeStack />

The stack is described at `harness/UX.md §7 "Persistence layers"`. L0–L1
are CC-native (user memory, `CLAUDE.md`). L2 (wiki) is defined by feature
`harness-llm-wiki`. L3 (research notes) is explicit-read only — never
auto-surfaced — so it can host long-form proofs without burning every
session's context budget.

---

## 2. Anatomy of a wiki entry

Every entry under `harness/wiki/<slug>.md` carries a fixed frontmatter
schema. Hover any field to see its role; the invariants are enforced by
`harness/wiki/SCHEMA.md` and by the composite guard
(`harness/composite-guard.sh`, per **Article VI**).

<WikiEntrySchema />

The schema deliberately includes *provenance* (`created`,
`last_verified`, `sources`) alongside *matching* (`triggers`) and
*decay* (`half_life_days`) — revisable knowledge must know its own age.
Bodies stay short (under ~40 lines); long-form rationale lives in
`harness/research/*.md` with a back-link.

---

## 3. Live keyword matcher

This is the core mechanic. The SessionStart hook tokenizes the operator's
initial message, scores every wiki entry by trigger overlap, and injects
the top-3 as `<system-reminder>` blocks. Try editing the textarea below —
the constellation redraws live, and cards re-rank by hit count.

<KeywordMatcher />

Backed by `scripts/harness/wiki_match.sh` (see feature
`harness-llm-wiki` §"Minimal viable implementation" step 2). The cap of
three keeps the surfaced-context cost bounded; the 4th-Nth matches are
reachable via `/harness:wiki-search` but never auto-injected. This is
the same "no in-loop flooding" discipline **Article III** (HITL
boundary) imposes on `AskUserQuestion`.

---

## 4. Half-life & re-verification

Wiki entries are *revisable*, not permanent. Each carries
`half_life_days`; after `last_verified + half_life_days`, the entry
still surfaces — Article VII permits stale knowledge — but with a
`restale-due` flag that prompts re-verification on the next loop that
cites it.

<HalfLifeTimeline />

The zones map directly to the reporter's behavior:

- **fresh** (0-50% hl) — surfaced silently.
- **warming** (50-100% hl) — surfaced with a green check.
- **restale-due** (>100% hl) — surfaced with a stale banner; the
  post-loop reporter (`cc-post-loop-slash`) prompts the operator to
  either bump `last_verified` or edit the body.
- **purge candidate** (>150% hl) — operator-only decision, via
  `/harness:wiki-add --cull`. Deletion follows the amendment template
  of **Article IX** so the culled knowledge lineage survives in `git
  log`.

This decay model is why the wiki can't be replaced by a big CLAUDE.md:
CLAUDE.md has no notion of verification age.

---

## 5. Read / write flow

The wiki is bidirectional by design (feature `harness-llm-wiki`,
`axis2: pre-loop (primary) + post-loop (secondary)`). The tab below
toggles between the two halves:

<ReadWriteFlow />

Both flows respect **Article III**: the operator interacts only at
pre-loop SessionStart (read side, via the hook) and post-loop
(`/harness:wiki-add`, write side). The in-loop body never calls
`AskUserQuestion` about the wiki — the operator's carve-outs are
**graduated-confirm** (`harness-graduated-confirm`, L2 irreversible
ops) and **pause/resume** (`harness-pause-resume`). The wiki simply
surfaces and is cited; citations are logged by
`gcli-agent-run-telemetry` for audit.

**Article VIII** applies too: the commit of each accepted wiki entry
is an iteration artifact. `loops/NNN-<slug>/wiki-refs.md` records what
the loop read *and* wrote, so the knowledge lineage is recoverable
from `git log` even years later.

---

## 6. Integration map

The wiki sits at the center of six other features. None of the
integrations is optional — each solves a problem the wiki inherits from
its axis position (outer / pre-loop primary, post-loop secondary).

<FeatureCrossRef />

The most load-bearing edges:

- **`cc-post-loop-slash`** hosts the write mechanic. Without it, no
  entries are ever proposed.
- **`gcli-agent-run-telemetry`** closes the usage-audit loop — every
  `see wiki:<slug>` citation inside an iteration is recorded.
- **`harness-rip-test`** runs the rippable probe: migrate one entry
  into CC-native memory, start a fresh session, check whether the
  entry surfaces without prompting. If it does, feature `harness-llm-wiki`
  is absorbed upstream and the outer scripts become dead weight
  (**Article II**).
- **`voyager-skill-library`** is deliberately *non-overlap*: skills are
  authored procedures, wiki entries are facts. Mixing them produces the
  "skill catalog as knowledge base" anti-pattern described in the
  research note's §Contrast.

---

## 7. Rippable signal

Per **Article II**, every feature must declare the empirical signal
that tells the operator it has been absorbed upstream. For
`harness-llm-wiki`:

> Migrate one wiki entry (e.g. `cad-path-2-room-temp.md`) into CC's
> native memory primitive. Start a fresh session, send a message
> containing two trigger keywords. If CC surfaces the entry unprompted
> with a cite-able source, **rip**.

The `tc_script` at `harness/tests/harness-llm-wiki.sh` runs this probe
automatically on every CC version bump. Passing = still needed;
failing = time to delete the outer scripts and the
`.claude/skills/wiki-surface/` directory.
