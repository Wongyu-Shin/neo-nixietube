# Harness UX Flow — Entry → Completion

> How an operator kicks off a loop, what the agent does between
> iterations, and what the agent hands back at the end. This page is
> the interactive companion to <code>harness/UX.md</code> and
> <code>harness/CONSTITUTION.md</code>.

The diagram below is the spine of the whole page: the green column is
the **pre-loop** (HITL active, Articles III + V), the amber ring is
**in-loop** (HITL forbidden except the two carve-outs in Article III),
and the violet column is **post-loop** (HITL active, Articles VII +
VIII). Hover any node for a preview; click to lock the side panel.

<HarnessFlowDiagram />

## 1 · Article III — the HITL boundary

The single most load-bearing invariant in the harness. **Active
human-in-the-loop input is legal only in pre-loop and post-loop.** The
in-loop segment is machine-driven and forbids <code>AskUserQuestion</code>
outright. The post-hoc transcript linter flags any violation.

<HITLBoundaryChart />

There are exactly two in-loop carve-outs, both specified in Article III
clause (c):

- **L2 graduated-confirm** for irreversible operations
  (`cc-hook-guardrail` deny + `harness-graduated-confirm` prompt).
- **Ctrl+C** for emergency stop, which is lossy by design.

Everything else happening inside the amber ring — iterating, committing,
verifying, guarding, logging — is agent-driven with zero interactive
prompts. Operator hints arrive asynchronously via `/harness:send` and
are consumed at the next atomic-step boundary.

## 2 · Pre-loop — scaffold, clarify, plan

Three slash commands staircase the operator from a raw intent to an
executable plan:

<CommandCheatsheet />

- **`/harness:new-loop <slug>`** (feature `harness-loop-scaffold`,
  Article VIII): auto-increments NNN, copies `loops/TEMPLATE/`, opens
  `spec.md` for Goal/Scope/Metric entry, and wires the loop to the
  Article III–VII invariants.
- **`/harness:clarify`** (feature `harness-clarify-gate`, Article V):
  walks the seven coverage dimensions D1–D7 and writes
  `clarifications.md`. Unresolved `[ASSUMPTION]` markers for D1 fail
  `composite-guard` (Article VI) — the loop cannot proceed.
- **`/autoresearch:plan`** (feature `plan-mode-discipline`, Article V):
  consumes `clarifications.md`, dry-runs verify, records the baseline.
  `ExitPlanMode` is the **only** legal HITL gate into in-loop (Article III).

## 3 · In-loop — the eight-phase ring

Each iteration walks eight phases in order. Phase 1 loads the
Constitution, surfaces wiki entries by keyword (Article VII), and
re-reads prior `reflexion` notes. Phases 2–5 ideate, modify, commit,
verify and guard. Phase 6 decides keep/discard/rework gated by
`composite-guard` (Article VI). Phase 7 writes one log line and the
milestone block via `harness-progress-cadence`. Phase 8 repeats, or
exits on a stop condition.

Ratcheting is performed by `noise-aware-ratchet` with a `SUM=MAX`
ratchet so LLM-as-judge noise (±10) never weakens the anchor. Plateau
detection by `plateau-detection` combines `ratchet-patience` and
`trend-slope` under the measured σ; tripping either is insufficient.

## 4 · In-loop safety tiers

Operations inside the ring are not homogeneous. The graduated-confirm
ladder classifies each op and decides whether it may run silently, ping
the statusline for 30 seconds, or block the loop entirely until the
operator acks it with a keystroke.

<SafetyTierLadder />

L2 events are appended to `report.mdx` and shown in the statusline. A
loop that hits L2 three times in five iterations auto-escalates to
operator attention regardless of per-event verdict.

## 5 · Stop conditions — automatic and manual

A loop ends for one of six reasons, split between the agent and the
operator.

<StopConditionMatrix />

Automatic stops (bounded N, goal-achieved, plateau) all run the
post-loop report cleanly. Manual pause and abandon preserve the
checkpoint. Emergency stop (Ctrl+C) is the only lossy path and should
be avoided when `/harness:abandon` or `/harness:pause` are available.

## 6 · Post-loop — report and wiki

Once the ring has exited, two post-loop slash commands hand the loop's
output back to the operator.

- **`/harness:report`** (feature `cc-post-loop-slash`, Article VIII):
  renders `report.mdx` with TL;DR, axis-coverage delta, kept/reworked/
  discarded experiments with commit hashes, L2 confirmation log, wiki
  contributions, and next steps.
- **`/harness:wiki-add`** (feature `harness-llm-wiki`, Article VII):
  the agent proposes 0–N keyword-triggered entries, the operator
  approves/edits/rejects each, and accepted entries land under
  `harness/wiki/<slug>.md`. A `wiki-refs.md` in the loop directory
  records **both** the wiki entries read during the loop and the ones
  it wrote, so future readers can trace the knowledge lineage in
  either direction.

Optional post-loop audits attach on demand: `llm-as-judge-audit` (with
self-enhancement-bias control via a different judge model),
`cross-domain-transfer-metric` on a held-out paired domain, and
`gcli-eval-compare-primitive` for paired A/B vs. a baseline ref.

## 7 · Persistence layers — the LLM-wiki question

"How does LLM-wiki persistence actually work?" turns out to have a
four-layer answer, of which only one (the harness wiki) is
simultaneously keyword-triggered, project-scoped, committable, and
half-life tracked.

<PersistenceLayers />

Article VII names this layer as the sole project-scoped cross-loop
knowledge store. `CLAUDE.md` is equivalent in scope but floods every
session, and user memory is cross-repo. Research notes live next door
to wiki entries but require explicit reads; the wiki's SessionStart
matcher is what makes the difference.

## 8 · Per-loop artifact tree

Every completed loop leaves the same tree under `loops/NNN-<slug>/`.
Hover a node for what it is, which command writes it, and which
Article governs it.

<ArtifactTree />

`results.tsv` is gitignored because it is reconstructable from the
commit series (Article VIII — "git is memory"). Everything else is
committed: spec, clarifications, plan, checkpoints, report, and the
two-directional wiki-refs.
